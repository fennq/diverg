Probationary Contract — Graphics Designer

This Probationary Contract (“Contract”) is entered into as of _______________ (“Effective Date”) between Diverg (“Company”) and _______________ (“Employee”).

1. Role and probationary period

Employee is engaged as a Graphics Designer on a one (1) week probationary period commencing on the Effective Date. This period is for the mutual evaluation of fit, capability, and working relationship.

2. Assessment criteria

During the first week, the Company will assess Employee’s:

  • Efficiency — quality and speed of deliverables;
  • Time management — adherence to deadlines and responsiveness;
  • Productivity — volume and consistency of output;
  • Reliability — attendance, communication, and follow-through.

Success in the probationary period is at the sole discretion of the Company and may lead to discussion of ongoing engagement, compensation, and bonus.

3. Compensation during probation

The first week of the probationary period is unpaid. No salary, fee, or other compensation is due for work performed during this one-week period. If the Company decides to continue the engagement after probation, the parties will discuss and agree in writing on potential pay and bonus before any further work is performed.

4. Confidentiality and non-disclosure (NDA)

Employee agrees to keep strictly confidential all information relating to the Company, its business, products, technology, clients, projects, and any work performed under this Contract. Employee shall not disclose, discuss, or communicate any such information to any person or entity other than the Company’s designated representative (the undersigned for the Company). This obligation applies both during and after the probationary period and survives termination of this Contract.

“Confidential information” includes but is not limited to: designs, concepts, strategies, internal processes, technical details, and any other non-public information learned in the course of engagement. Breach of this clause may result in immediate termination and, where applicable, legal recourse.

5. Term and termination

The probationary period ends seven (7) days after the Effective Date. Either party may end the engagement at any time during or at the end of the probationary period without notice. At the end of the week, the Company will inform Employee of its decision and, if applicable, propose terms for continued engagement including compensation.

6. General

This Contract constitutes the entire agreement between the parties. Amendments must be in writing. If any provision is held unenforceable, the remainder remains in effect. This Contract is governed by the laws of the jurisdiction in which the Company operates.
